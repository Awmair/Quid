Quid reviewed **100 public senior living community website journeys across 28 operator groups and 23 US states** on July 12 to 14, 2026. We followed only what a family could see without submitting a form. We did not pose as a family, test response times, or inspect any operator's CRM, inbox, staffing, conversion rate, or resident data.

The benchmark shows two realities at once. Many websites still place a manual step between a high-intent visitor and a confirmed visit. Larger operator templates more often expose preferred-time forms or embedded inquiry assistants, but automation and staff follow-up frequently appear together.

## Direct answer: what happens after a senior living tour CTA?

In this purposive 100-page benchmark, **62 community journeys exposed a manual or delayed-coordination signal**. Examples included a form that did not visibly reserve the requested time, a preferred-date request, or language indicating that staff would arrange or confirm the next step.

At the same time, **47 community pages exposed an automation signal**, usually an embedded inquiry assistant. These findings overlap: 14 pages showed both automation and a visible staff-follow-up or preferred-time step.

The practical conclusion is not that every manual signal means a slow team, or that every script means a completed booking. It is that the words “Schedule a Tour” do not reveal what happens next. Operators should test the entire visible journey: acknowledgement, real availability, confirmation, staff ownership, human handoff, and stalled-work visibility.

## What the public journey can and cannot prove

A website can show friction without proving poor internal performance. A community may route its form into an excellent CRM, respond within minutes, and coordinate tours reliably. Likewise, the presence of an automated assistant does not prove that a tour was booked, that the system integrates with the operator's record system, or that every conversation is handled appropriately.

This benchmark therefore uses restrained language:

- **Manual or delayed signal** means the public journey visibly leaves coordination, confirmation, or follow-up for a later step.
- **Automation signal** means the page exposes an embedded automated inquiry experience or a clearly automated tour journey.
- **Both** means automation is present while the visitor still sees staff follow-up or another non-final step.
- **Unknown** means the public page does not provide enough evidence for a confident classification.

The benchmark does not measure reply speed, lead quality, tour attendance, move-ins, occupancy, revenue, staffing competence, or clinical care.

## The revenue problem in plain words

Families may contact more than one community. Every unnecessary pause between inquiry and a clear next step gives another community an opening. The fix is not “remove the admissions team.” The fix is to stop making staff recreate predictable coordination by hand.

A controlled workflow should acknowledge the request, expose only approved tour availability, confirm the selected visit, notify the staff owner, pause for sensitive questions, and show managers what is still stuck. ## Methodology

### Sample construction

This July 2026 edition combines two purposive cohorts:

1. **25 independent and small-operator pages** previously evaluated during Quid's outreach research on July 12 and 13. These rows emphasize independent communities and operators with roughly one to 10 locations. Community and staff identities remain anonymized in the public row-level file.
2. **75 multi-location pages** reviewed on July 14. We selected 15 community pages from each of five operator clusters using official public sitemaps: <a href="https://www.brookdale.com/en.sitemap.xml" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Brookdale</a>, <a href="https://www.sunriseseniorliving.com/sitemap.xml" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Sunrise Senior Living</a>, <a href="https://www.benchmarkseniorliving.com/sitemap.xml" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Benchmark Senior Living</a>, <a href="https://www.merrillgardens.com/sitemap.xml" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Merrill Gardens</a>, and <a href="https://www.leisurecare.com/sitemap_index.xml" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Leisure Care</a>.

All 75 named expansion URLs returned a public page during review. The source register includes the page title, state, classification, review date, and exact URL.

### Review protocol

- **Unit of analysis:** one public community page and its visible inquiry or tour controls.
- **Geography:** 23 US states across the combined sample.
- **Operator clustering:** 100 pages represent 28 operator groups; one shared template may affect multiple rows.
- **Evidence captured:** CTA category, visible journey category, manual/delayed signal, automation signal, state, operator-size band, and review date.
- **Safety:** no form submission, impersonation, resident information, medical information, email collection, or private-system access.
- **Quality control:** every expansion URL was fetched successfully and classified from its public HTML on July 14, 2026.

### Limitations

This is not a probability sample and must not be cited as the share of all US senior living communities. The original cohort intentionally emphasized possible workflow gaps. The expansion intentionally sampled multiple pages from five large operators, so repeated templates are part of the observation. Public HTML can expose scripts and form language but cannot prove implementation quality, availability, response time, integration, or conversion performance.

The benchmark will retain this URL. Future editions will publish the new denominator, dates, operator mix, methodology changes, and archived downloads rather than silently changing the findings.

## What senior living teams can check today

Open your own website on a phone and follow the journey without using staff knowledge to fill in the gaps.

- Does the family know the request arrived?
- Are they choosing a supported time or merely requesting one?
- Is the visit visibly confirmed?
- Who owns the next step?
- What happens after hours?
- Does automation pause when the family replies or asks a sensitive question?
- Can a manager see tomorrow which inquiries are still stuck?
