Aline vs ECP: Senior Living CRM Comparison for 2026

What is the main difference between Aline and ECP CRM?

Both Aline CRM and ECP CRM can serve as senior living systems of record. Aline publicly presents a wide connected platform across growth, resident, financial and operational products; ECP emphasizes an assisted-living sales cycle flowing into its clinical and operating platform.

What are Aline and ECP CRM?

What is Aline?

Aline CRM, Aline Connect, Aline Discover, Aline Nurture and related platform products

Senior living and post-acute operators seeking connected sales, marketing, resident, financial and operational workflows. Aline CRM is a system of record and action within the wider Aline platform.

Aline Connect publicly describes instant calling and texting for new inquiries, while Aline CRM captures and manages leads. Tour coordination is publicly described across Aline CRM, Contact Center and marketing capabilities. Aline publicly describes CRM analytics, customizable reports and broader operational insight products.

How do Aline and ECP CRM compare in admissions work?

Inquiry response and tour coordination

Aline: Aline Connect publicly describes instant calling and texting for new inquiries, while Aline CRM captures and manages leads. Tour coordination is publicly described across Aline CRM, Contact Center and marketing capabilities.

ECP CRM: ECP publicly describes lead capture from web, phone and referral sources. ECP publicly confirms tour scheduling with calendar sync, Office 365 integration and reminders.

Follow-up channels and lead nurturing

Prospect records, referrals and reporting

Aline: Aline CRM is a system of record and action within the wider Aline platform. Aline publicly offers referral-management capabilities, including a Smart Referral CRM for skilled nursing. Aline publicly describes CRM analytics, customizable reports and broader operational insight products.

ECP CRM: ECP CRM is publicly described as a purpose-built system for prospects and the admissions pipeline. Referral-source lead capture and source attribution are publicly described. Pipeline visibility, source attribution and portfolio reporting are publicly described.

Integrations, operations and human escalation

Aline: Aline publicly states it has more than 100 integrations and an open ecosystem. The wider platform spans growth and engagement, resident living, financials and operations. Aline Connect publicly describes handing qualified prospects to a community team or contact center.

ECP CRM: Office 365, calendar integration and a developer portal are public; exact integrations should be confirmed. ECP connects CRM with a broader platform that includes EHR, eMAR, billing and resident operations. ECP publicly describes a clinical handoff in its assisted living sales cycle.

These statements describe public vendor positioning. Buyers should ask both vendors to demonstrate the exact workflow, because a publicly described product category does not establish a specific integration, configuration, or escalation rule.

Where might each option fit better?

Which option is more appropriate for a single assisted living community?

A single community should compare the exact modules it needs, existing care systems and migration burden. ECP may align with an ECP clinical environment; Aline may align with operators wanting its CRM and connected growth ecosystem.

Which option is more appropriate for a multi-community operator?

Both vendors publicly address multi-community visibility. A portfolio should compare data governance, reporting layers, operational modules, integrations and administration in a live demonstration.
